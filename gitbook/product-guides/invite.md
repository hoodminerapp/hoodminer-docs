# 🤝 Rally the Merry Men

Robin Hood was nothing without his band. Neither is HoodMiner — the game grows when players bring players. The referral system pays you for exactly that, instantly and on-chain.

## How it works

1. Open the dApp and connect your wallet — your **personal referral link** is generated automatically.
2. Share it anywhere: Twitter, Telegram, group chats, your trading circle.
3. When someone deposits through your link, the contract **instantly sends you 12% of their deposit in ETH**.

No claiming step, no pending balance, no waiting for a payout run. The referral reward is executed by the smart contract inside the *same transaction* as the deposit. If their deposit confirms, your reward has already arrived.

## The details

* **Rate:** 12% of every deposit made through your link — not just the first one. Your referral keeps paying every time they top up.
* **Paid in:** ETH, directly to your wallet.
* **Tiers:** **One level only.** You earn from the players you personally invite — never from *their* invites. HoodMiner is not a multi-level scheme, and we consider that a feature: every reward in the system traces back to one real invitation.
* **Limits:** None. Invite one friend or one thousand. What you earn is yours — withdraw it, or deposit it back into Hoods and let it compound.

## Why single-tier?

Multi-level referral pyramids look generous and drain pools fast — the deeper the tiers, the more of every deposit leaks to people who did nothing. One honest tier keeps the referral budget meaningful (12% is high *because* it is single-level) and keeps the pool healthy for the players actually in the game.

{% hint style="warning" %}
**Anti-abuse:** self-referrals between your own wallets are wash trading against yourself — you pay the 5% deposit fee to collect 12% of your own money and gain nothing but gas costs. The math is intentionally not worth gaming.
{% endhint %}

{% hint style="info" %}
Referral rewards come from the same contract balance that pays Loot claims. A referral reward is only as good as the pool behind it — which is exactly why the system rewards *bringing in* deposits rather than extracting them.
{% endhint %}
